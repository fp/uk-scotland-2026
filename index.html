Day 02Sun · 26 Jul

London — your one big day

Base: London · two coherent tracks — pick one or blend.

  • Track A
    Royal & historic London
    Westminster (Parliament & Big Ben for photos), then St James’s Park to Buckingham Palace, The Mall to Trafalgar Square, and the free National Gallery (open Sundays). Finish in Covent Garden among the performers and market stalls; a West End show in the evening if you book ahead.
    Sunday-sensitive: Westminster Abbey is for worship on Sundays, not tourist visits — attend a service or view the exterior, and save the interior for a weekday. Changing the Guard runs only on selected days — confirm the 26 Jul schedule before building the morning around it.
  • Track B
    Museums & Sunday markets
    Columbia Road Flower Market (Sunday-only, ~8 AM–3 PM) and Brick Lane in the East End, then a graze lunch at Borough Market (open Sun). Afternoon in one great free museum — the British Museum or riverside Tate Modern.
Eat

Dinner The Wolseley (grand European café — book), Borough Market stalls, or a Soho institution. Reserve anything sit-down for Sunday evening.

Isle of Skye Highland Games

Base: Glengarry Castle Hotel · leave ~6:45–7 AM

  • By 9:30 AM
    Skye Highland Games — The Lump, Portree ↗
    One of Scotland's oldest gatherings, held on The Lump above Portree harbour since 1877 — caber toss, tug-of-war, solo piping, Highland dancing, plus a hill race and yacht race. A procession leaves Somerled Square at 9:45; local events start 10:00, open events 11:45, running to ~5 PM. Tickets booked — 2 adults. Arrive early; parking and seating fill fast, and bring wet-weather gear. Keep it games-only; home by ~8 PM.

Day 13Thu · 6 Aug

Move east — Culloden & Glenlivet

Check out Glengarry (11 AM) → check in Craigellachie Hotel (3 PM) · Hotel info →

A restored 1893 hotel above the meeting of the Fiddich and Spey, in the heart of malt-whisky country. It's home to the Quaich Bar — 700-plus single malts — plus the 300-year-old Spey Inn pub and the GEAMAIR restaurant, with the Macallan and a dozen distilleries a short drive away. The Speyside Way footpath and the landmark Craigellachie Bridge are on the doorstep.

  • Late morn
    Culloden Battlefield ↗
    The windswept moor where the 1746 battle ended the Jacobite rising in under an hour. The National Trust visitor centre lays out both sides of the story, and clan grave-markers stand out on the field itself. Allow ~2 hrs.
    Learn more →
  • Afternoon
    The Glenlivet Distillery ↗
    Your first Speyside dram — the first licensed distillery in the parish (1824), set in a remote glen once notorious for illicit stills. Book the tour ahead; drivers can take their tasting away in miniatures. Then on to the hotel.
    Tours & booking →
Eat

Lunch The Gather'n Café (Aberlour) on arrival, or the café at Glenlivet en route.

Dinner In-house at the Craigellachie — GEAMAIR's tasting menu, or the 300-year-old Spey Inn pub.
